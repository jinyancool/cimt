## Project Overview

Carotid artery plaque serves as a pivotal indicator of atherosclerosis, prominently reflecting the susceptibility to atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD). Utilizing carotid artery imaging for assessing carotid intima-media thickness (CIMT) holds paramount importance in the timely identification of atherosclerosis. However, the relationship between CIMT and cardiovascular risk factors across different age and gender groups remains poorly defined.

### Methods

Lowess regression served as the method for regression analysis, and Spearman’s correlation was employed for correlation analysis. XGBoost was employed to predict CIMT within each age segment. Dynamic programming techniques were applied to identify the optimal model group.
